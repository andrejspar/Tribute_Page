Leopold Ružička

The Nobel Prize-winning chemist

Timeline of Leopold Ružička

1887
September 13: Born in Vukovar, Croatia (then part of Austria-Hungary).
1906
Enrolled at the Technical University in Karlsruhe, Germany.
1910
Received his diploma in technical chemistry.
1912
Earned his Ph.D. under Hermann Staudinger at the Technical University of Karlsruhe, focusing on synthetic and structural organic chemistry.
1912-1916
Worked as Staudinger's assistant, continuing research in organic chemistry.
1916
Accepted a position at the Geneva-based Sandoz Company (now Novartis).
1917-1920
Moved to Zurich, Switzerland, and began collaborating with Leopold Ruzicka on the chemistry of natural products.
1921
Became a Swiss citizen.
1923
Appointed as a lecturer (Privatdozent) at the University of Zurich.
1927
Became a full professor at the University of Utrecht, Netherlands.
1929
Returned to Switzerland to accept a position at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETH) in Zurich.
1934
Conducted significant research on the structure of terpenes and steroids, leading to major advancements in organic chemistry.
1939
Nobel Prize in Chemistry: Awarded jointly with Adolf Butenandt for their work on polymethylenes and higher terpenes.
1940-1957
Continued his research at ETH Zurich, mentoring numerous students and making further contributions to organic chemistry.
1957
Retired from active teaching and research but remained involved in scientific advisory roles.
1976
September 26: Passed away in Mammern, Switzerland.
